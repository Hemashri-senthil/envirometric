Water Quality Index (WQI) Overview

What is WQI?

The Water Quality Index (WQI) is a numerical representation of water quality. It helps people understand how clean or polluted the water is based on several parameters like pH, Dissolved Oxygen, Nitrate, BOD, and more. The index simplifies complex water data into a single value that represents water condition: Excellent, Good, Medium, Bad, or Very Bad.

Sources of Water Pollution

Major sources of water pollution include industrial discharge, agricultural runoff, domestic waste, oil spills, and untreated sewage. These pollutants enter lakes, rivers, and groundwater, degrading water quality and affecting aquatic life and human health.

Common Water Pollutants

Some common pollutants include:

  • pH imbalance
  • Dissolved Oxygen (DO)
  • Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD)
  • Nitrate and Nitrite
  • Heavy Metals like Lead and Mercury
These pollutants negatively impact aquatic ecosystems and pose risks to human health.
